HOW BB84 WORKS
Why can't Eve eavesdrop without being detected?
The quantum no-cloning theorem prevents Eve from copying a qubit without disturbing it. When she intercepts and re-sends, she must guess the correct basis. If she guesses wrong (50% of the time), she introduces errors. When Alice and Bob compare a sample of their key bits over a classical channel, they see an error rate of ~25% — far above the expected noise — revealing the eavesdropper.
What are the two bases?
The rectilinear basis (⊕) encodes 0 as horizontal polarization and 1 as vertical. The diagonal basis (⊗) encodes 0 as +45° and 1 as −45°. Alice randomly chooses a basis for each bit. Bob randomly chooses a basis to measure. If they use the same basis, Bob gets the correct bit. If they use different bases, Bob gets a random result and those bits are discarded.
Is BB84 actually used in practice?
Yes. Commercial QKD systems based on BB84 and related protocols are deployed by companies including ID Quantique, Toshiba, and others. China's Micius satellite demonstrated satellite-based QKD over 1,200 km. The main limitation is distance — fiber attenuation limits range to a few hundred kilometers without quantum repeaters.
