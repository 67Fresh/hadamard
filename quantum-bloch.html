UNDERSTANDING THE BLOCH SPHERE
What does every point on the sphere represent?
Every point on the surface of the Bloch sphere represents a unique pure qubit state. The north pole is |0⟩, the south pole is |1⟩, and the equator contains all equal superpositions. The interior represents mixed states (statistical mixtures). For a pure qubit state |ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^(iφ)sin(θ/2)|1⟩, θ is the polar angle and φ is the azimuthal phase.
What do the Pauli gates do geometrically?
Every single-qubit gate is a rotation of the Bloch sphere. X (quantum NOT) rotates 180° around the X axis, flipping |0⟩ to |1⟩. Y rotates 180° around Y. Z rotates 180° around Z, flipping the phase. Hadamard (H) rotates 180° around the diagonal X+Z axis, mapping |0⟩ to |+⟩. S and T are quarter- and eighth-turn rotations around Z.
Why can't the Bloch sphere represent entangled states?
The Bloch sphere only describes a single qubit in isolation. When qubits become entangled, you cannot describe each qubit independently — the system lives in a joint Hilbert space that can't be factored into individual qubit states. That's what makes entanglement so strange and powerful.
